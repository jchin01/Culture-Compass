Communication

  • When greeting someone in America, it is ideal to offer to shake hands as this is the form in which we say hello.
  • American people do not like too little space between themselves and their speaking partners, and they do not like to touch or be touched while having a conversation.
  • If we see Americans who form a circle with their thumb and index finger, they refer to something good, this signal means "OK," "yes," or "fine".
  • Yawning means tiredness, boredom, and it is impolite.
  • High five means "congratulations."
  • Rubbing the hands together means that the person is cold or feels impatient anticipation.
  • Another generally used gesture is whistling which is quite often used at sport events, performances, to gain attention from someone, or watching a pretty woman in the street.
  • In America, a thumbs up actually signifies "good job" or approval of something.
  • Do not stick out a middle finger as it expresses hatred for someone and is considered extremely offensive.
  • While in other countries tips are considered offensive due to the fact it may seem as an added incentive for doing their job, in America, tips are given out generously.

Table Manners

  • Arriving Late is expected as Americans usually run behind schedule and the standard.
  • Altering a meal does not portray as rude, it is standard to ask for condiments in an American table setting.
  • Do not rest your elbows on a table.
  • The fork is held in the left hand facing down while the knife is held in the right hand.
  • Social meals are more about eating while chatting and talking hours over the food.
  • Feel free to refuse specific foods or drinks without offering an explanation.
  • Napkins, if provided, are placed on the lap.
  • A toast might take place at the start of a formal meal or for a special occasion/guest.
  • Americans socialise and do business over breakfast, lunch, and dinner.

Miscellaneous

Popular Festivals

  • Art Basel displays leading galleries and artwork from all around the world. Located in Miami Beach, Florida, the festival usually takes place around December and lasts for a week.
  • The Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival is an annual music and arts festival held at the Empire Polo Club in Indio, California. It's duration last for about two weeks.
  • Electric Daisy Carnival, commonly known as EDC, is one of the biggest electronic dance music festivals in the world with its flagship held annually every June in Las Vegas, Nevada.
  • San Diego Comic-Con International is a multi-genre entertainment and comic convention held annually in San Diego, California.
  • The San Francisco Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, and Transgender Pride Celebration, usually known as San Francisco Pride, is a parade and festival held at the end of June each year in San Francisco, California, to celebrate the lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) people and their allies.
