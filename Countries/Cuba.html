Communication

  • Men shake hands when greeting one another and usually maintain direct eye contact. Handshakes may linger. Good friends and family will engage in a firm hug which may be combined with a few slaps on the back.
  • At a first meeting, women generally shake hands. Friends and close acquaintances kiss each other once on the cheek.
  • Cubans tend to be direct and some what louder than what the norm is in North America.
  • Cubans prefer standing close to one another while talking with someone they know. One to two feet is normal, but when meeting strangers, this distance is farther.
  • There is a fair amount of touching between people while conversing (between men and men, women and women, and men and women).
  • Cubans tend to speak very quickly and loudly. They use their hands and bodies for emphasis when speaking and tend to be emotionally expressive.
  • Cubans will point by puckering their lips in the direction or person they are referring to.
  • Wrinkling or scrunching up of your nose usually means, "huh" or "what?".

Table Manners

  • If eating in a nice restaurant or a local's home, dress a little nicer than you otherwise would especially if dining with a business colleague. Try to arrive on time, but remember many locals will arrive up to 15 minutes late.
  • When guests are present, hosts will usually make a point of offering second helpings, but it is acceptable to decline.
  • The meal may begin with a toast, the most common toast being "salud" (health) and the meal will begin with the words "buen provecho" (have a good time eating).
  • While eating use the continental style (knife in the right hand, fork in the left) for all foods, including fruits; keep your hands in sight be resting your wrists on the edge of the table.
  • Once you are finished eating, place your fork and knife together on the right side of your plate to indicate you are finished.

Miscellaneous

Attire

  • The dress code in Cuba is relatively casual because of its level of development combined with its tropical (humid) Caribbean climate.
  • Shorts and bikinis are appropriate at the beach, particularly if in a resort or more touristy area. In general, Cubans dress according to the climate though not overly provocative.
  • Shorts or light trousers with short sleeve shirts or tank tops are generally fine.
